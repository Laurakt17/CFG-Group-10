Welcome to Glasgow!

Glasgow is a port city on the River Clyde in Scotland's western Lowlands.

It's famed for its Victorian and art nouveau architecture, a rich legacy

of the city's 18th – 20th-century prosperity due to trade and shipbuilding.

Today it's a national cultural hub, home to institutions including the

Scottish Opera, Scottish Ballet and National Theatre of Scotland, as well

as acclaimed museums and a thriving music scene.

Hidden Gems!

Dockyard Social

Hip weekend food court with DJs, themed bars & street food eats, including Thai & Greek options.

Oro Restaurant

Award winning Italian Restaurant based in the heart of the Southside of Glasgow.

Mowgli Street Food

Situated in City Centre, try Mowglis delicious Indian Street food.

Glasgow University

The University is often compared to Hogwarts so it is a must see for any Harry Potter fan

Glasgow Necropolis

The Necropolis is a Victorian garden cemetery full of wonderful architecture.

Glasgow Cathedral

Glasgow Cathedral is the oldest cathedral in mainland Scotland and the oldest building in Glasgow.

Pollok Park

The park is Glasgow's largest park and the only Country Park within Glasgow.

Tennents Brewery

One of Scotland's oldest ongoing businesses, this brewery Scotland's favourite beer.

Park Lane Market

Market held on the first and last Sunday of each month featuring boutique stalls!
