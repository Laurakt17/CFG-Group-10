Welcome to Brighton

Ever since royal party animal George IV fell in love with Brighton back in the 18th Century, the city has been a magnet for visitors in search of fun, frolics and fresh sea air. With Regency squares, the iconic Royal Pavilion and piers, eight miles of beach, art, live music, entertainment, independent shops, boutique hotels and award-winning restaurants, Brighton is well-known for being a welcoming and diverse city with a passion for culture. Read on for our favourite spots and top tips!

See the sights

  • The Lanes enjoy a plethora of independent shops and eateries
  • Royal Pavilion George IV's opulent and quirky seaside party escape
  • Sea Life Centre Victorian architecture and fascinating sea creatures
  • Palace Pier food, rides, arcades and fun for everyone

Hidden gems

  • Brighton Museum and Art Gallery explore world-class art, history and design
  • Brighton Dome multi-arts venue for live music, comedy, dance, theatre and more
  • Duke of York's Picturehouse one of the oldest cinemas in the world
  • The Upside Down House a unique photo experience that defies gravity
